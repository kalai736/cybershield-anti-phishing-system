    // 02 Attack Vectors

    How phishing actually reaches you

    Different bait, same goal: get you to hand over credentials, money, or access.

    Spoofed email

    A sender name you recognize, a domain that's one character off, and a request that can't wait until tomorrow.

    Cloned login pages

    A pixel-perfect copy of a real sign-in page, hosted on a lookalike domain, built only to capture what you type.

    Manufactured urgency

    "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours." Pressure is the payload — it short-circuits the pause you'd normally take.

    Malicious attachments

    An invoice, resume, or shipping label that quietly installs malware or macros the moment it's opened.

    Vishing & smishing

    The same tricks by phone call or text — a "bank" asking you to confirm a code, or read back an OTP you just received.

    Impersonation & pretexting

    A message posing as your CEO, IT desk, or a coworker, built around a story convincing enough that you skip verification.

    // 04 Defense Kit

    Habits that shut phishing down

    None of these require special tools — just a five-second pause before you act.

    HOVER

    Preview before you click

    Hover any link to see its real destination in your status bar before trusting it.

    VERIFY

    Confirm through a second channel

    Call or message the sender directly using a number you already trust — not one from the email.

    MFA

    Turn on multi-factor authentication

    A stolen password alone shouldn't be enough to get into any of your accounts.

    REPORT

    Flag it, don't just delete it

    Reporting to your IT/security team or email provider helps block the same attack for others.

    PAUSE

    Let urgency be your first red flag

    Real deadlines rarely require you to skip normal verification steps.

    UPDATE

    Keep software patched

    Many attacks rely on known vulnerabilities that a routine update would have closed.
